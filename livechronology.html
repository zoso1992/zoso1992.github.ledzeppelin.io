The chronology listed below is a complete listing of all confirmed concert dates played by Led-Zeppelin beginning in late 1968 until the fall of 1980. From 1970 until the bands end Led-Zeppelin were the world's top musical attraction, and by the end of 1969, after having been a group for a little over a year ceased all use of support or co-headlining bands. Following 1969 the band also avoided festival performances, save for a few instances like the Bath festival of 1970 and the headlining of Knebworth in 1979. Preferring to perform their own concerts where their own schedule could be followed and outside sound engineers unfamiliar with the band could not interfere. The same principal was applied to television performances, as most performances were mimed at the time and the few live performances would be at the mercy of television producers not accustomed to working with a rock band, let alone one as dynamic as Led-Zeppelin, as a result only a couple of television performances were ever done and only done in the first year of the bands existence. Manager Peter Grant along with Page maintained a stance that Led-Zeppelin would be seen live and in concert or not at all.
